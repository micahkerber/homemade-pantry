Texas LLC Formation Plan

Eight steps from name confirmation to open bank account.
Prepared April 2026  ·  Jaime Kerber  ·  Brookshire, TX
1 Confirm the Name ~30 min  ·  ~$1
  • Search Texas SOS SOSDirect — confirm "The Homemade Pantry LLC" isn't already registered in Texas: direct.sos.state.tx.us
  • Search USPTO Trademark Database — confirm no federal trademark exists: tmsearch.uspto.gov (search "homemade pantry")
  • If both come back clear, proceed to Stage 2
2 File the LLC with Texas 1–2 hours  ·  $300
  • Go to sos.state.tx.us and file Form 205 — Certificate of Formation
  • Business name: The Homemade Pantry LLC
  • Business purpose: "Culinary experience and gathering services"
  • Registered office address: 2415 Sweetwood Ct, Brookshire TX 77423
  • Pay the $300 filing fee online
  • Save the stamped Certificate of Formation — you'll need it for the bank
What a registered agent is: Texas requires every LLC to name a registered agent — a person who officially receives legal paperwork on behalf of the business. Jaime can be her own registered agent using the home address. Simple and free.
3 Get Your Federal Tax ID (EIN) 15 min  ·  Free
  • Apply at irs.gov/ein — get it instantly online
  • This is the business equivalent of a Social Security number — required to open a bank account and file taxes
4 Create an Operating Agreement 1 hour  ·  Free
  • Texas doesn't legally require one — but every LLC should have it
  • Defines ownership, profit distribution, and what happens if the business changes
  • Since this is a single-member LLC, a simple template works — available free at legalzoom.com or rocketlawyer.com
  • Sign it and keep the copy with your business records — you do not file this anywhere
5 Open a Business Bank Account 1 hour  ·  Free
  • Bring your Certificate of Formation + EIN to a bank
  • Recommended: Chase Business Complete, or a local Texas credit union
  • Never mix personal and business money once the LLC is active — this is what keeps your personal assets protected legally
6 Business Insurance 1–2 days  ·  ~$500–$1,200/yr
  • Get General Liability Insurance before hosting any paying guests
  • Look into FLIP (Food Liability Insurance Program) — designed for food businesses, starts around $299/year: fliprogram.com
  • Ask your homeowner's insurance carrier if your policy covers home-based business activity — most don't, and you'll need a separate rider
7 Texas Food & Cottage Food Compliance 1–2 hours  ·  ~$10
  • Get a Food Handler Certificate — required for anyone handling food commercially in Texas: txfoodhandler.com (~$10, ~2 hours online)
  • Review Texas Cottage Food Law (Texas Health & Safety Code §437) — allows some home-based food businesses to operate without a commercial kitchen license
  • Call the Waller County Health Department to confirm what permits, if any, are required for in-home culinary experiences
  • Check Waller County / City of Brookshire zoning for any rules about guests coming to a home-based business
8 Annual Obligations (Ongoing) 1 hour/year  ·  Free
  • File the Texas Public Information Report annually with the Texas Comptroller — due May 15 each year, no fee for most small LLCs
  • Keep all business income and expenses in the business bank account and tracked in QuickBooks
