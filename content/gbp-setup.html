1

Create the Profile

Go to business.google.com and sign in

  1. 1
    Go to business.google.com Sign in with thehomemadepantryco@gmail.com
  2. 2
    Click "Manage now" or "Add your business" If Google finds an existing listing for your address, claim it instead of creating a new one.
  3. 3
    Enter your business name Type exactly: The Homemade Pantry
  4. 4
    Choose a business category Search for and select "Cooking school" — it's the closest match. You can add more categories after setup.
  5. 5
    When asked "Do you want to add a location customers can visit?" Select No — your guests come to you by private invitation, not a storefront.
  6. 6
    When asked if you serve customers outside your location Select Yes — this unlocks the service area option and lets you hide your address.

3

Enter & Hide Your Address

Required for verification — but kept off your public listing

  1. 1
    Enter your business address when prompted This is for Google's verification process — it will NOT appear publicly if you complete the next step.
  2. 2
    Look for the checkbox: "Hide my address" It may be labeled "I serve customers at their location" or "Don't show my address." Check this box.
  3. 3
    After setup, double-check in Info settings Go to your profile → Edit profile → Business location — confirm "Show address" is toggled OFF.
⚠️ If you skip hiding the address, your home address will appear on Google Maps. Always verify this setting before finishing setup.

Verify the Business

Google has to confirm the business is real — this is the waiting step

📮 Postcard Verification

Google will mail a postcard to 2415 Sweetwood Court, Brookshire, TX 77423 containing a 5-digit PIN. The postcard is not visible to the public — it's just Google's way of confirming someone real is at that address.

  • Arrives in 5–7 business days (sometimes up to 14)
  • When it arrives: go to business.google.com → click "Verify" → enter the PIN
  • Your listing goes fully live after PIN entry
  • Do not request a second postcard unless the first one hasn't arrived after 14 days
While You Wait for the Postcard
  • Your listing is live in a limited state — Google just won't rank it prominently yet
  • Add any missing photos or details now
  • Set up your Q&A section — add the most common questions yourself (see below)
  • Link your Instagram @thehomemadepantry in the social profiles section

Seed the Q&A Section

Google lets businesses post and answer their own questions — do this before anyone else does

Add These Questions (ask & answer them yourself)
  1. Q
    How many people can attend? Up to 6 guests per evening — the experience is intentionally intimate. Some customizations are available on request.
  2. Q
    What do guests take home? Everything you make — a mini sourdough loaf, flavored butter, small-batch jam, an active starter in a mason jar, and a printed recipe card.
  3. Q
    Do I need baking experience? None at all. Everything is hands-on and guided from start to finish. It's an evening, not a class.
  4. Q
    How do I book? Visit www.homemadepantry.com to submit a booking inquiry. A 50% deposit is required to hold your evening.
  5. Q
    What is your cancellation policy? Cancellations 7 or more days before the event receive a full deposit refund. Cancellations within 7 days are non-refundable, but we're happy to reschedule.
8

After Verification — Keep It Active

Google rewards profiles that are regularly updated

  • Reply to every review — especially the first few. Thank guests by name.
  • Post updates monthly — new photos, seasonal offerings, upcoming dates. Google Posts appear on your profile.
  • Ask guests to leave a review — the day-after thank-you email (via HoneyBook) is the perfect moment to request one.
  • Update hours when your availability changes — outdated hours are the #1 reason customers lose trust.
  • Confirm address stays hidden — check this every few months. Google occasionally prompts businesses to confirm or update their address, and the setting can accidentally get changed.
