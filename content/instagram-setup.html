Instagram Setup Guide

Everything Jaime needs to launch @thehomemadepantry

1

Create the Account

Do this on Jaime's phone — takes about 5 minutes.

  • 1
    Download Instagram from the App Store or Google Play if it's not already installed.
  • 2
    Tap "Sign up" → use email: thehomemadepantryco@gmail.com → create a strong password and save it somewhere safe.
  • 3
    Set your username to @thehomemadepantry — check it's available. If taken, try @thehomemadepantryco or @homemadepantry.tx
  • 4
    Switch to a Professional account: Go to Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → choose Business → Category: Kitchen & Cooking or Local Business.
  • 5
    Add contact info: In your profile settings, add email thehomemadepantryco@gmail.com. This shows a "Contact" button on your profile.
  • 6
    Keep the account private until all 9 posts are uploaded and the profile is complete. Then flip to public.
💡 A Business account gives you access to Instagram Insights (analytics), the ability to run ads later, and a contact button — all things you'll want as bookings grow.

After You Go Public

Rules to follow from day one.

  • ✓
    Reply to every comment within 30 minutes of posting — especially on the first few posts. The algorithm rewards engagement right after publishing.
  • ✓
    Post Stories every day even on days you don't post to the feed. Behind-the-scenes, a quick thought, a photo of the kitchen — anything.
  • ✓
    Post 4–5x per week max. Consistency beats volume. Missing a day is fine. Going silent for two weeks hurts.
  • ✓
    Reels first. Instagram pushes Reels to people who don't follow you yet. Even a short 15-second clip of bread coming out of the oven will outreach any photo post.
  • ✓
    DMs are bookings. Anyone who comments or DMs "how do I book?" gets a warm, personal response — not a link. Talk to them like a person.
